
Task 5 – Part A: Cybercrime Case Study (Phishing)

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity. It usually occurs through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives an email or message claiming to be from a bank, online service, or government authority. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or unauthorized activity. It includes a link that directs the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the original. When the user enters their login credentials or financial information, the attacker captures this data.

Students and young internet users are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify sources carefully. The consequences of phishing can be severe, including financial loss, unauthorized transactions, identity theft, and loss of access to personal accounts.

For example, a student might receive a message claiming to be from their bank asking them to verify their account details. Trusting the message, they enter their information on a fake website, leading to unauthorized access and withdrawal of funds.

Phishing attacks continue to increase due to lack of awareness, making it essential for users to stay cautious and informed while interacting online.
